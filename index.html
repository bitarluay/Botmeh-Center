Discover Botmeh

Nestled in the heart of Lebanon's Chouf region, Botmeh Center stands as a gateway to one of the country's most enchanting mountain villages.

Botmeh, meaning "the terebinth tree" in Syriac and Arabic, is a historic locality perched at 950-1200 meters above sea level, just 55 kilometers from Beirut. Located southeast of the renowned Beiteddine Palace, this charming village spans over 200 hectares of pristine mountain landscape.

A Living Heritage

The village is renowned for its:

  • Ancient Architecture: Traditional stone houses that tell stories of generations past
  • Historic Church: A spiritual landmark visible from our building's balconies
  • Natural Wonders: The legendary "Oak of Oum Al-Charatit," ancient pine woods, and geological caves
  • Archaeological Treasures: Phoenician-era remains, ancient sarcophagi, and fascinating animal and plant fossils
  • Traditional Crafts: Historic grape presses and old springs that sustained the community for centuries

With nearly 1,500 residents, Botmeh maintains its authentic charm while offering modern conveniences. Our center is strategically located on the main street, placing you at the heart of village life with stunning views of the historic church and surrounding cedar forests from your balcony.
